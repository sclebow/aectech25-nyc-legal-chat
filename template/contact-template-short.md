## ARTICLE 2: ARCHITECT'S RESPONSIBILITIES

### §2.1 Professional Services

The Architect shall provide professional services as set forth in this Agreement. The Architect represents that it is properly licensed in the jurisdiction where the Project is located.

### §2.2 Standard of Care

The Architect shall perform its services consistent with the professional skill and care ordinarily provided by architects practicing in the same or similar locality under the same or similar circumstances.

### §2.3 Representative

The Architect shall identify a representative authorized to act on behalf of the Architect with respect to the Project.

### §2.4 Conflict of Interest

Except with the Owner's knowledge and consent, the Architect shall not engage in any activity that would reasonably appear to compromise the Architect's professional judgment with respect to this Project.

### §2.5 Insurance Requirements

#### §2.5.1 Commercial General Liability

Policy limits of not less than **$1,000,000** for each occurrence and **$2,000,000** in the aggregate for bodily injury and property damage.

#### §2.5.2 Automobile Liability

Policy limits of not less than **$1,000,000** per accident for bodily injury, death, and property damage.

#### §2.5.3 Primary and Excess Coverage

The Architect may achieve required limits through a combination of primary and excess or umbrella liability insurance.

#### §2.5.4 Workers' Compensation

At statutory limits.

#### §2.5.5 Employers' Liability

Policy limits not less than **$1,000,000** each accident, **$1,000,000** each employee, and **$1,000,000** policy limit.

#### §2.5.6 Professional Liability

Policy limits of not less than **$1,000,000** per claim and **$1,000,000** in the aggregate.

#### §2.5.7 Additional Insured Obligations

The Architect shall cause the primary and excess or umbrella policies for Commercial General Liability and Automobile Liability to include the Owner as an additional insured.

#### §2.5.8 Certificates of Insurance

The Architect shall provide certificates of insurance to the Owner that evidence compliance with these requirements.

## ARTICLE 3: SCOPE OF ARCHITECT'S BASIC SERVICES

### §3.1 Basic Services Overview

The Architect's Basic Services consist of those described in this Article 3.

#### §3.1.1 Project Management

The Architect shall manage the Architect's services, research applicable design criteria, attend Project meetings, communicate with members of the Project team, and report progress to the Owner.

#### §3.1.2 Coordination

The Architect shall coordinate its services with those provided by the Owner and the Owner's consultants. The Architect shall be entitled to rely on the accuracy, completeness, and timeliness of services and information furnished by the Owner and the Owner's consultants.

#### §3.1.3 Schedule

The Architect shall submit for the Owner's approval a schedule for the performance of the Architect's services, including allowances for the Owner's review and approvals.

#### §3.1.4 Owner Directives

The Architect shall not be responsible for an Owner's directive or substitution made without the Architect's written approval.

#### §3.1.5 Governmental Authorities

The Architect shall contact governmental authorities required to approve the Construction Documents and entities providing utility services to the Project.

#### §3.1.6 Filing Documents

The Architect shall assist the Owner in connection with filing documents required for approval of governmental authorities.

### §3.2 Design Phase Services

#### §3.2.1 Review

The Architect shall review the program and other information furnished by the Owner, and shall review laws, codes, and regulations applicable to the Architect's services.

#### §3.2.2 Scope of Services

See Attachment A for complete scope of services.

## ARTICLE 4: ADDITIONAL SERVICES

### §4.2 Architect's Additional Services

The Architect may provide Additional Services after execution of this Agreement. Any Additional Services shall entitle the Architect to compensation pursuant to Section 11.3 and an appropriate adjustment in schedule.

#### §4.2.1 Notice and Authorization

Upon recognizing the need for Additional Services, the Architect shall notify the Owner and shall not proceed until receiving the Owner's written authorization for the following:

1. Services necessitated by a change in the Initial Information or a material change in the Project
2. Services necessitated by the enactment or revision of codes, laws, or regulations
3. Changing or editing previously prepared Instruments of Service necessitated by official interpretations
4. Services necessitated by decisions of the Owner not rendered in a timely manner
5. Preparing digital models or other design documentation for transmission
6. Preparation of design and documentation for alternate bid or proposal requests
7. Preparation for, and attendance at, a public presentation, meeting or hearing not included in scope
8. Preparation for, and attendance at, a dispute resolution proceeding or legal proceeding

## ARTICLE 6: COST OF THE WORK

### §6.1 Definition

The Cost of the Work shall be the total cost to the Owner to study all elements of the Project designed or specified by the Architect.

### §6.2 Estimates

In preparing estimates of the Cost of Work, the Architect shall be permitted to include contingencies and to determine materials, equipment, and construction types. The Architect's estimate shall be based on current area, volume or similar conceptual estimating techniques.

### §6.3 Budget Adjustments

If the Architect's estimate exceeds the Owner's budget, the Architect shall make appropriate recommendations to adjust the Project's size, quality, or budget, and the Owner shall cooperate in making such adjustments.

The Architect grants to the Owner a nonexclusive license to use the Architect's Instruments of Service solely for purposes of constructing, using, maintaining, altering and adding to the Project, provided the Owner substantially performs its obligations under this Agreement.

#### §7.3.1 Owner's Use Without Architect

## ARTICLE 8: CLAIMS AND DISPUTES

### §8.1 General

#### §8.1.1 Statute of Limitations

The Owner and Architect shall commence all claims and causes of action within the period specified by applicable law, but in any case not more than **10 years** after the date of Substantial Completion.

#### §8.1.2 Waiver of Damages

To the extent damages are covered by property insurance, the Owner and Architect waive all rights against each other except such rights to insurance proceeds.

#### §8.1.3 Consequential Damages

The Architect and Owner waive consequential damages for claims, disputes, or other matters arising out of or relating to this Agreement.

### §8.2 Mediation

#### §8.2.1 Condition Precedent

Any claim or dispute shall be subject to mediation as a condition precedent to binding dispute resolution.

#### §8.2.2 Administration

Mediation shall be administered by the American Arbitration Association in accordance with its Construction Industry Mediation Procedures.

#### §8.2.3 Costs

The parties shall share the mediator's fee and filing fees equally. The mediation shall be held where the Project is located.

#### §8.2.4 Binding Dispute Resolution

If mediation does not resolve the dispute, the method of binding dispute resolution shall be:

**[X] Arbitration** pursuant to Section 8.3 of this Agreement
[ ] Litigation in a court of competent jurisdiction
[ ] Other

### §8.3 Arbitration

#### §8.3.1 Administration

Arbitration shall be administered by the American Arbitration Association in accordance with its Construction Industry Arbitration Rules.

#### §8.3.1.1 Timing

A demand for arbitration shall be made no earlier than concurrently with the filing of a request for mediation.

#### §8.3.2 Enforceability

The agreement to arbitrate shall be specifically enforceable in any court having jurisdiction.

#### §8.3.3 Award

The award rendered by the arbitrator(s) shall be final, and judgment may be entered upon it in any court having jurisdiction.

#### §8.3.4 Consolidation or Joinder

Either party may consolidate an arbitration or include persons or entities by joinder under specified conditions.

### §8.4 Survival

The provisions of this Article 8 shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE 9: TERMINATION OR SUSPENSION

### §9.1 Suspension by Architect

If the Owner fails to make payments, the Architect may suspend services after giving seven days' written notice.

### §9.2 Suspension by Owner

If the Owner suspends the Project, the Architect shall be compensated for services performed prior to suspension and for expenses incurred in interruption and resumption.

### §9.3 Termination by Architect

If the Owner suspends the Project for more than 90 cumulative days, the Architect may terminate this Agreement by giving not less than seven days' written notice.

### §9.4 Termination for Cause

Either party may terminate this Agreement upon not less than seven days' written notice should the other party fail substantially to perform.

### §9.5 Termination for Convenience

The Owner may terminate this Agreement upon not less than seven days' written notice for the Owner's convenience and without cause.

### §9.6 Compensation Upon Termination

If terminated for convenience or by the Architect, the Owner shall compensate the Architect for services performed prior to termination, Reimbursable Expenses incurred, and costs attributable to termination.

### §9.7 Termination Date

This Agreement shall terminate one year from the date of Substantial Completion.

### §9.8 Rights to Instruments

The Owner's rights to use the Architect's Instruments of Service in the event of termination are set forth in Article 7 and Section 9.7.

## ARTICLE 10: MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

### §10.1 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the law of the place where the Project is located. The Federal Arbitration Act shall govern Section 8.3.

### §10.2 Terms

Terms in this Agreement shall have the same meaning as those in AIA Document A201-2017, General Conditions of the Contract for Construction.

### §10.3 Binding Effect

The Owner and Architect bind themselves, their agents, successors, assigns, and legal representatives to this Agreement. Neither party shall assign this Agreement without written consent of the other.

### §10.4 Third Parties

Nothing in this Agreement shall create a contractual relationship with, or a cause of action in favor of, a third party.

### §10.5 Hazardous Materials

The Architect shall have no responsibility for hazardous materials or toxic substances at the Project site.

### §10.6 Promotional Materials

The Architect shall have the right to include photographic or artistic representations of the design among promotional and professional materials. The Owner shall provide professional credit for the Architect in promotional materials.

### §10.7 Confidential Information

If the Architect or Owner receives information designated as "confidential" or "business proprietary," the receiving party shall keep such information strictly confidential.

#### §10.7.1 Exceptions

The receiving party may disclose confidential information after 7 days' notice when required by law, arbitrator's order, or court order, or to employees, consultants, or contractors for the Project.

### §10.8 Severability

The invalidity of any provision shall not invalidate the Agreement or its remaining provisions.
